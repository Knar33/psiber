Cairn

cairn [kairn]
noun
a heap of stones set up as a landmark, monument, tombstone, etc.

Summary

I am building a world and an app in Hyperfy called Cairn. It will be a multiplayer virtual web experience where authenticated users can move and stack a collection of rocks on an island. The rocks will be locked in place after a period of time forming a final piece of collaborative artwork. NFTs will be distributed to users who participate in the world which I plan to incorporate into my future project.

Design

Cairn will be built as a [Hyperfy] world with a custom app
Users can authenticate by signing in with an Ethereum wallet which contains a Hyperfy world or a Webaverse Genesis Pass.
Authenticated Users can click on a stone and it will go into their hand if:

  1. there are no stones on top of it
  2. the user has no stone in their hand
Authenticated users with a stone in their hand can click to place the stone down at the cursor location if:
  1. the surface normal is facing upwards (you have to obey gravity)
Users that log out with a stone in their hand drop it on the ground

I will release an NFT collection of stones and airdrop free tokens to people who participate. The stones will be procedurally generated gltf files which I'm hoping I can do entirely on-chain. If I can't manage that I will generate them off-chain and release a static collection.

Development

There are two components I need to build for this project. The first is the Hyperfy app which will handle the 3d aspect and be a front-end experience for the user. I will also need a web server with a database to handle the user interactions with the rocks and persist state between visits. Hyperfy has no persistent state API so I need to do this myself.

The database will have 3 tables: Users, Rocks, and RockHistory. The tables will look something like this:

Users:
| UserID | EthereumAddress | LastLogin |

Rocks:
| RockID | HeldByUser | PositionX | PositionY | PositionZ | ParentID |

RockHistory:
| RockHistoryID | RockID | UserID | Event |
